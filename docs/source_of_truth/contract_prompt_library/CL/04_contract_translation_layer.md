# Contract Translation Layer

The translation layer exists to bridge article analysis and actual contract relevance.

## Contract thesis
Bullish CL usually means the market is repricing tighter physical balances, stronger near-term demand, more credible supply disruption, stronger product pull-through, or a supportive macro backdrop that outweighs dollar drag. Bearish CL usually means looser balances, weaker demand, inventory pressure, OPEC+ skepticism, refinery weakness, or macro and dollar conditions that compress crude expectations.

## Mandatory outputs
- net impact on Crude Oil Futures (CL / MCL): bullish / bearish / mixed / no edge
- primary driver hierarchy, ranked
- whether the best expression is this contract or a different one such as products or equities
- expected reaction horizon
- underpriced / consensus / stale assessment
- confirmation markers
- invalidation markers
- trade-use note describing how the output should shape bias or setup preference without dictating execution

## Clean translation rule
If the article cannot be translated into a contract-specific mechanism with internal consistency, output **no edge**.
