# Deep Analysis Protocol

## Objective
Convert the article into a disciplined assessment of whether it changes crude-oil pricing logic, not whether it sounds persuasive.

## Required steps
1. Extract the article's core claim.
2. Separate confirmed facts, plausible inference, speculation, and opinion.
3. Identify what is genuinely new versus recycled background.
4. Classify the article as primary reporting, synthesis, or commentary.
5. Map the article into the relevant channels:
   - physical supply
   - demand and growth
   - inventories and storage
   - refinery and product-market spillover
   - shipping, sanctions, and logistics
   - dollar and macro risk
6. Explain the transmission mechanism step by step.
7. State the strongest competing interpretation.
8. Judge whether the information is underpriced, already priced, mixed, or impossible to assess from text alone.
9. Split likely impact by horizon: overnight, pre-open, pit/RTH opening phase, same-session continuation, 1-3 day swing.
10. End with confirmation, invalidation, and a trade-use note.

## Hard rule
If the article cannot survive a clean mechanism test, output **no edge**.
