# Article Selection Protocol

Select articles only when they can plausibly alter crude-oil pricing through one or more of these channels:
- OPEC+ policy, compliance, quotas, or political cohesion
- physical supply disruption, sanctions, war-risk premium, shipping chokepoints, or weather-driven outages
- inventory, refinery, pipeline, export, import, or storage dynamics
- product-market stress that can feed back into crude demand or run rates
- global growth and demand expectations
- dollar, liquidity, or macro risk transmission that is strong enough to matter for crude

## Prioritize
- primary reporting with new facts
- official statements from OPEC, IEA, EIA, API, producers, refiners, or government authorities
- article clusters where narratives conflict or where the market move looks larger than the article quality justifies

## De-prioritize
- generic commodity-roundup articles
- opinion pieces with no new facts
- articles that merely restate price action after the move
- broad macro commentary unless the transmission into crude is explicit and strong

## Output requirement
For every selected article, state:
- why it was selected
- which crude-oil channel it plausibly affects
- whether it looks like a catalyst, a context piece, or post hoc narrative attachment
