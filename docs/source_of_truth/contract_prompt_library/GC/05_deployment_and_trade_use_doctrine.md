# Deployment and Trade-Use Doctrine

Use the workflow in four windows:

## 1. Pre-event or pre-session
Run it before major data, policy communication, auctions, or significant overnight narrative shifts that plausibly affect Gold Futures (GC / MGC).

## 2. Immediately after a meaningful article or headline cluster
Use it to distinguish genuine repricing from post hoc media explanation.

## 3. Before acting on a narrative you already believe
Use it as a thesis stress test, not a confirmation machine.

## 4. After a large move, before chasing
Use it to decide whether the move is durable repricing, late narrative attachment, a fade candidate, or a no-trade.

## Do not use it as primary decision logic when
- price action is clearly dominated by microstructure or positioning
- the article is commentary with no new information
- the workflow output conflicts with obvious market rejection
- you are looking for exact entries, stops, or size

## Operator doctrine
The workflow should inform:
- relevance
- directional bias
- horizon
- setup preference
- what must confirm

The workflow should not dictate:
- exact entry
- exact stop
- exact size
- override of clear price rejection
