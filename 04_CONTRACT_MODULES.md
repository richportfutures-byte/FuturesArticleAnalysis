# Contract Modules

## Shared pattern across all five contracts
All five modules share the same block sequence:
1. Article selection
2. Narrative clustering and screening
3. Deep analysis
4. Contract translation
5. Deployment and trade-use doctrine
6. Confirmation and invalidation
7. Pre-trade SOP
8. Post-article reaction SOP
9. Single-article one-shot prompt
10. Multi-article one-shot prompt
11. Quick intraday filter
12. Domain appendix

All modules also share the operator rule that these workflows shape bias, relevance, and scenario framing rather than act as autonomous trade directives.

## NQ

### Primary objective
Translate articles into NQ-relevant directional bias, leadership implications, and time-horizon-aware setup preference without letting narrative override price and structure.

### Core channels
- US rates and real yields
- megacap tech leadership
- semiconductors and AI capex
- broad risk sentiment and liquidity
- earnings and growth expectations

### Article-selection discipline
Include first:
- primary reporting with new information
- policy, macro, or market-structure articles tied directly to yields, megacap tech, semis, AI capex, breadth, liquidity, and risk appetite
- clusters where multiple outlets are forcing a narrative onto the same move
- pieces that may change timing, durability, or mechanism of the market response

Down-rank:
- generic opinion columns
- pure recap articles with no new facts
- emotional or thematic narratives unsupported by market mechanism
- articles whose relevance cannot be traced to NQ in one or two clean steps

### Selection rubric
Score each article from 1 to 5 on:
- novelty
- source quality
- contract relevance
- timing sensitivity
- explanatory mechanism clarity

Only push articles into deep analysis when the combined score implies actual decision value rather than intellectual interest.

### Narrative-cluster rules
For multi-article mode, the module must:
- group by event, policy catalyst, or price move
- identify what each article claims is new
- separate dominant narrative from article-specific additions
- flag cases where writers describe the same move with different causes
- classify the cluster as discovery, consensus, or post hoc storytelling
- reject clusters that do not clearly route through at least one core NQ channel

### Deep-analysis requirements
The NQ module must:
- extract the article's core claim
- separate facts, inference, speculation, and rhetoric
- identify what is genuinely new versus recycled context
- map first-order and second-order effects
- explain the transmission mechanism into NQ
- steelman the strongest opposing interpretation
- decide whether the article changes price expectations, path expectations, or merely narrative framing
- assign a horizon: headline reaction, same-session, multi-session, or context only
- state what evidence would confirm or invalidate the read

### Mandatory translation outputs
- net impact on NQ: bullish / bearish / mixed / no edge
- primary driver hierarchy, ranked
- whether the best expression is this contract or a different one
- expected reaction horizon
- underpriced / already priced / mixed / impossible-to-assess / stale assessment
- confirmation markers
- invalidation markers
- trade-use note explaining how the output should inform but not dictate trading

### Contract thesis discipline
Bullish NQ means lower or stable yields with supportive tech leadership, AI/semis confirmation, and risk appetite that survives the cash open. Bearish NQ means rising yield pressure, weaker leadership, semis underperforming, or macro news that directly compresses long-duration equity multiples.

### Best deployment windows
- pre-event or pre-session before major data, policy communication, or significant overnight narrative shifts
- immediately after a meaningful article or headline cluster
- before acting on a narrative already believed
- after a large move, before chasing

### Active-hours and catalyst windows
Structural liquidity:
- 9:30 AM to 11:00 AM ET: cash open and index-component pricing
- 3:00 PM to 4:00 PM ET: close and settlement
- 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM ET: midday lull
- 3:00 AM to 6:00 AM ET: European open

Event-driven liquidity:
- 8:30 AM ET: primary US macro releases
- 10:00 AM ET: secondary US macro releases
- 2:00 PM to 2:30 PM ET Wednesdays: FOMC decision and press conference
- 4:00 PM to 4:15 PM ET during earnings season: megacap tech earnings

### Highest-value insight
Whether the article truly changes rate pressure, tech leadership, semis / AI capex demand, or earnings / growth expectations in a way that should alter NQ bias.

### Least valuable use
Single-article trading during microstructure-driven chop, dealer-gamma pinning, or when article logic conflicts with clear breadth and leadership deterioration.

### Confirmation markers
- 10Y yields and real-yield direction
- QQQ and SOX relative strength
- NVDA / MSFT / AAPL leadership quality
- breadth and equal-weight behavior
- cash-open acceptance versus rejection

### Invalidation logic
Downgrade or invalidate when:
- the supposed transmission channel fails to appear in the relevant cross-market markers
- NQ reacts in the opposite direction and gains acceptance there
- the move looks technical while the supposed macro mechanism remains absent
- later information weakens the article's novelty or factual basis

### Post-reaction review requirements
After the move, log whether the article functioned as catalyst, rationalization, or noise, and whether the event should be retained as continuation fuel, fade fuel, or future ignore material.

### Output should shape
- directional bias
- leadership watchlist
- continuation versus fade preference
- what must confirm before a setup deserves attention

### Key disqualifiers
- reject narratives that cannot be traced to a clean NQ transmission mechanism
- if the article cannot be translated into a contract-specific mechanism with internal consistency, output `no_edge`
- do not use the workflow as primary decision logic when price action is clearly dominated by microstructure or positioning

### One-shot output contract
Single-article runs must output:
- executive signal
- what is genuinely new
- mechanism map
- competing interpretation
- pricing assessment
- horizon split
- confirmation and invalidation
- HOW THIS SHOULD INFORM TRADING, NOT DICTATE TRADING
- final verdict with confidence and actionability 1-10

Multi-article runs must output:
- source quality map
- common facts and disputed claims
- driver ranking
- contract translation
- pricing / timing assessment
- confirmation and invalidation
- HOW THIS SHOULD INFORM TRADING, NOT DICTATE TRADING
- final verdict with confidence and actionability

## ZN

### Primary objective
Translate articles into Treasury-price implications with strict price-versus-yield discipline and explicit separation of outright direction from curve and supply effects.

### Core channels
- Fed-path repricing
- inflation-growth decomposition
- curve context
- auctions
- issuance
- fiscal credibility
- term premium
- risk-off demand

### Best deployment windows
- pre-data
- post-data
- post-auction
- after fiscal/Fed narrative shifts
- after a large move to decide continuation vs fade vs no trade

### Highest-value insight
Whether the article changes Fed path, inflation-growth expectations, or term-premium pressure.

### Least valuable use
Generic equity/business commentary without Treasury transmission.

### Confirmation markers
- cash yields and futures price moving consistently
- 2s/10s or 5s/10s curve response
- Fed-funds path repricing
- auction tone and dealer absorption
- equity risk-off versus inflation-selloff distinction

### Additional workflow requirements from detailed blocks
- build article-by-article source map
- mark claims as confirmed, plausible, weakly supported, or unsupported
- rank top 3 drivers
- assess whether the narrative is early, consensus, or stale
- steelman the best alternative interpretation

### Key disqualifier
Do not use article narratives to override yield rejection, auction failure, or curve behavior that implies a different mechanism.

## CL

### Primary objective
Convert energy and macro articles into disciplined, contract-specific relevance for crude oil so that supply, demand, geopolitics, inventory, refinery, and product-market effects are ranked instead of blurred into generic “oil up” or “oil down” commentary.

### Core channels
- OPEC+ policy, cohesion, and compliance
- physical supply disruption or restoration
- inventories, storage, and balance trajectory
- refinery utilization, outages, and maintenance
- product pull-through and crack-spread behavior
- sanctions, shipping, and chokepoint logistics
- global growth demand and macro regime
- US dollar and broad risk sentiment

### Article-selection rules
Prioritize:
- primary reporting with new facts
- official statements from OPEC, IEA, EIA, API, producers, refiners, or government authorities
- article clusters where narratives conflict or where the move looks larger than article quality justifies

De-prioritize:
- generic commodity roundups
- opinion pieces with no new facts
- articles that merely restate price action
- broad macro commentary without explicit crude transmission

### Mandatory narrative buckets
- supply shock or disruption
- OPEC+ policy or quota enforcement
- inventory and storage narrative
- refinery or product-market narrative
- growth-demand narrative
- dollar or macro-liquidity narrative
- geopolitical premium narrative
- post-hoc explanation or low-information commentary

### Deep-analysis requirements
- identify the first actual new fact
- separate confirmed facts, inference, speculation, and opinion
- explain the mechanism step by step
- state the strongest competing interpretation
- decide whether the story is underpriced, already priced, mixed, or impossible to assess
- split impact by horizon

### Translation outputs
- net impact: bullish / bearish / mixed / no edge
- ranked driver hierarchy
- best expression vehicle
- expected reaction horizon
- underpriced / consensus / stale assessment
- confirmation markers
- invalidation markers
- trade-use note

### Confirmation markers
- front-month crude price acceptance and follow-through
- term-structure response: prompt strength vs deferreds
- Brent-WTI behavior when relevant
- inventories and subcomponents when thesis is inventory-led
- refinery utilization / runs / outages when thesis is refinery-led
- crack-spread behavior when product pull-through is central
- tanker / shipping / sanctions follow-through when logistics-led
- dollar behavior and broad risk sentiment when macro-linked

### Invalidation markers
- price rejects the narrative quickly and cannot hold the headline zone
- the curve does not confirm a supposedly tight physical story
- products fail to confirm a refinery or demand thesis
- official data contradicts the stated mechanism
- the move looks large but breadth of energy confirmation is poor

### Output should shape
- relevance
- directional bias
- horizon
- setup preference
- what must confirm

### Key disqualifier
If the mechanism is not internally consistent or the facts are not new, output no edge and downgrade actionability.
